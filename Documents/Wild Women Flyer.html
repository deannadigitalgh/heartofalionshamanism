About Deanna Stennett

Deanna Stennett is a Georgia Licensed Acupuncturist, Nationally Certified. She holds a Master’s Degree in Transformative Leadership and Social Change. She has studied Shamanism with the Foundation for Shamanic Studies, Sandra Ingerman, Tom Cowan, and is an

ordained minister. She offers rites of passage ceremonies such as weddings and funerals. Deanna led a journey/drumming group from 1997 until 2020 in Maryland. She closed the group when the pandemic hit. In 2021 she moved to the southern Appalachian mountains of North Georgia and lives in Morganton. She is offering Acupuncture in both Blairsville and Ellijay.

This past October, Ms. Stennett offered her first Shamanic: Learn to Journey course since the move. It was held at Mountain Light Unitarian Universalist Church (MLUUC) in Ellijay. It was well attended. And she co-lead a Solstice Celebration at MLUUC, where 38 people gathered to honor the return of the sun.

Deanna has taught eight Two-Year Training Programs in Shamanism, as well as many other workshops, including this workshop on Wild Women. This workshop is based on Clarissa Pinkola Estes’ recorded book Wild Women Archetypes.

Ms. Stennett has authored a book about her shamanic journeys to the spirits of the

Acupuncture points. It is written for the layperson and is available in paperback or kindle on Amazon. It’s called: Poetry of the Body: Stories about Acupuncture Points.

Wild Women Program

Deep inside you is a woman who longs to be free, wild, creative, imaginative and appreciated for those qualities. You may be old and have lived your life for everyone else, or you may be young and want to avoid “captivity”. Whatever stage of life you are in, it is never too late to tap into the animal, untamed wild part of yourself.

Women are very special creatures. Regardless of age, marital status, sexual orientation, race, religious upbringing we have many things in common and we can learn, grow and support each other in ways that our brothers cannot grasp.

Using the work of Clarissa Pinkola Estes from her recording on Wild Women Archetypes, we will explore our wild side, connect with our intuition and embrace our natural rhythms. We will do this through Shamanic Journeys, creative expression (including but not limited to song, dance, artwork and ceremony), and friendship/support building exercises.

If you are a woman and you are longing to be heard, understood, loved and fulfilled, then this program is for you. Walk the ancient paths of womanhood from the cultures and traditions of the wise, and discover your own power and wisdom.
