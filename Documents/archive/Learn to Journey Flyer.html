Deanna Stennett, L.Ac., MA, Nationally Certified, NCCAOM, is a Georgia licensed Acupuncturist with offices in Ellijay and Blairsville. She graduated from The Worsley Institute (nka The Academy for Five Element Acupuncture) in 1993, and has been practicing in Maryland until 2021 when she moved to GA. Deanna learned about Shamanism in Acupuncture School from a shaman, herbalist and acupuncturist by the name of Eliot Cowan, author of Plant Spirit Medicine. After acupuncture school she began studying with the Foundation for Shamanic Studies and completed their advanced 3 year program. She has been studying with Sandra Ingerman author of many books, including Soul Retrieval, Medicine for the Earth, and since 1995.

Deanna has been practicing and teaching Shamanism since 1995. She offers classes, workshops and healing sessions. Deanna has used Shamanic Journeying to meet with the spirits of the Acupuncture Points which her book Poetry of the Body: Stories About Acupuncture Points (available on Amazon) is about, written for the lay person.

Deanna Stennett is a lover of animals, nature, natural healing, art, poetry, and being a loving, compassionate healing presence on the Earth.

Shamanism is a spiritual practice that is not religiously affiliated with any one belief system. It is the oldest known spiritual healing system on the planet dating back 40-100 thousand years, based on archaeological studies.

Siberia is the home of the oldest shamanic tribe.

Everyone here in the US thinks that Shamanism comes from Native Americans, however the word “Shaman” comes from the Tungus tribe in Siberia. The word means “one who sees in the dark.” During the time of the Soviet Union, shamans were murdered and persecuted. Some of them escaped to the US where Michael Harner, the founder of the Foundation for Shamanic Studies learned from them, and used his organization to help re-establish their homeland after the fall of the USSR. I learned this type of shamanism from the Foundation when I was first studying it.

If you go far enough back, every culture on the planet came from tribes or villages that practiced shamanism, though called different things.

Come learn about the heritage you never knew about, and feel the awareness of all of nature around you from a different perspective.
